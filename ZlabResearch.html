Structure-Function Connectivity Relationships of the Brain in Adults

The purpose of the study is to investigate how people's behavior and brain activity are influenced by various cognitive and sensory factors. Specifically, we are interested in how perception, behavior, and neural activity may be influenced by eye movements. In order to investigate these topics, we use functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) and diffusion weighted imaging (DWI). Functional MRI (fMRI) and DWI have become major research tools in the developing science of brain and behavior. They are imaging methods that use a strong magnetic field and radio waves to make pictures of your body. fMRI is a type of specialized MRI scan used to measure the change in blood flow related to neural activity in the brain. DWI is a type of specialized MRI scan used to measure the direction/orientation of fibers in the brain; it tells us about how brain areas are connected to each other.
